Motherboard

The motherboard (MOBO) is the circuit board that acts as the central communication center for all the different hardware in your PC. Motherboards have a specific chipset that supports certain CPUs and sockets. Always double check what socket your CPU requires, it will determine if your motherboard is compatible.There are three MOBO sizes: ATX, Micro ATX, and Mini-ITX. They all vary in size and compatibility when it comes to other hardware connectivity, such as the GPU, RAM, and SATA ports. SATA ports are part of the current standard of connecting storage devices into the computer. Storage devices like the hard drive, external drives, and solid-state drives all use up a SATA port each, so make sure to accommodate for each port. Also take into consideration, the bigger the motherboard, the more components you can add.
